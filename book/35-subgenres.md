# 35 · Subgenres and their signatures

Acid isn't one thing. From its 1987 Chicago origin, it splintered into dozens of subgenres, each with its own conventions, tempos, and emotional registers. This chapter walks through the major ones and shows you how to produce each in acidflow.

The goal: hear a record and know "this is Hardfloor," then know how to make a Hardfloor-style track yourself.

## Acid house (Chicago, 1987-1989)

**Origin**: Phuture, *Acid Tracks* (1987). DJ Pierre, Spanky, Herb J.

**Tempo**: 120-125 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Pedal patterns (mostly on root).
- Sparse accents.
- Occasional octave jumps.
- Moderate RES (60-70%), moderate CUTOFF (40-60%).
- Filter sweeps over bars.

**Drum style**:
- 4-on-floor kick.
- Clap on beats 2 and 4 (no snare).
- Closed hats on offbeats.
- No open hats.

**FX**:
- Minimal. Maybe a touch of reverb.
- No delay usually.

**Arrangement**:
- Long intro, long main, minimal breakdown.
- The filter sweep IS the arrangement.

**Key records**: *Acid Tracks*, *Phuture - We Are Phuture*, *Adonis - No Way Back*, *DJ Pierre - Generate Power*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Classic.
- Pattern archetype: Pedal.
- BPM: 122.
- Drums: kick (1,5,9,13), clap (5,13), closed hat (3,7,11,15).
- FX: zero or minimal.
- Performance: slow filter sweep over 4-8 minutes.

## UK acid house (1988-1990)

**Origin**: The Second Summer of Love. A Guy Called Gerald, Baby Ford, 808 State.

**Tempo**: 120-128 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Melodic patterns (not just root).
- Slides and accents balanced.
- Moderate filter motion.
- Sometimes paired with chord stabs (from other synths — can't do this in acidflow alone).

**Drum style**:
- Kick on every beat.
- Snare AND clap on backbeat often (layered).
- Open hats on 8 and 16.
- Closed hats on offbeats.
- More elaborate than Chicago.

**FX**:
- Moderate delay (1/8).
- Some reverb.

**Arrangement**:
- Standard verse-chorus-breakdown structure.
- More pop-leaning than Chicago.

**Key records**: *A Guy Called Gerald - Voodoo Ray*, *808 State - Pacific State*, *Baby Ford - Oochy Koochy*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Classic or Melodic.
- Pattern archetype: Melodic.
- BPM: 124.
- Drums: full kit with snare + clap layered.
- FX: delay 1/8, mix 20-30%, feedback 0.4-0.5.

## Detroit techno-acid (1988-1992)

**Origin**: Derrick May, Kevin Saunderson, Juan Atkins. Also the New York-Detroit axis: Frankie Bones, Joey Beltram.

**Tempo**: 125-135 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Dense patterns. Every step plays.
- Strong accents.
- Less reliance on slides than UK acid.
- CUTOFF opens wider (60-80%).

**Drum style**:
- Faster, denser.
- 16th-note hats sometimes.
- Kick heavier, more aggressive.

**FX**:
- Drive moderate.
- Delay minimal.
- Reverb tight.

**Arrangement**:
- Aggressive builds.
- Short breakdowns.

**Key records**: *Joey Beltram - Energy Flash*, *Frankie Bones - Looney Tunes*, *Derrick May - Strings of Life* (not acid per se but Detroit-era).

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Driving.
- Pattern archetype: Driving.
- BPM: 132.
- Drums: 16th-note hats, snare + clap on backbeat.
- FX drive: 30%.

## Hardfloor / acid trance (1992-1996)

**Origin**: Hardfloor (Ramon Zenker, Oliver Bondzio), Union Jack, Astral Projection.

**Tempo**: 135-150 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Layered. Typically TWO OR THREE 303 lines running in parallel (not possible in acidflow alone).
- High RES, low CUTOFF at start, opening up.
- Heavy accents and slides.
- Self-oscillating frequently.

**Drum style**:
- Driving kick, every beat or syncopated.
- Snare on backbeat.
- 16th-note hats.
- Occasional open hat accents.

**FX**:
- Heavy drive.
- Tempo-synced delay.
- Moderate reverb.

**Arrangement**:
- Long builds.
- Dramatic drops.
- Extended breakdowns.
- 7-9 minute tracks.

**Key records**: *Hardfloor - Acperience 1*, *Hardfloor - Trancescript*, *Union Jack - Two Full Moons and a Trout*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Squelch.
- Pattern archetype: Driving.
- BPM: 140.
- Drums: full techno kit, 16th hats.
- FX drive: 50-70%.
- FX delay: 1/8 dotted, mix 40%, fb 0.5.

For the layered 303 sound, export one line, reload, program a different line, export, mix in a DAW.

## Ambient acid / IDM-acid (1993-1998)

**Origin**: Aphex Twin, Plastikman (Richie Hawtin), Luke Vibert.

**Tempo**: 100-130 BPM (variable).

**303 style**:
- Sparse, melodic, sometimes atonal.
- Long decays.
- Filter motion is the main event.
- Low RES (doesn't scream).

**Drum style**:
- Minimal. Sometimes just kick. Sometimes no drums.
- When drums present: sparse, often half-time.

**FX**:
- Heavy reverb.
- Tape delay.
- Drive varies.

**Arrangement**:
- Experimental. Not verse-chorus.
- Slowly evolving.
- 6-15 minute tracks.

**Key records**: *Plastikman - Spastik*, *Plastikman - Helikopter*, *Aphex Twin - Analogue Bubblebath*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Dubby.
- Pattern archetype: Dub.
- BPM: 120 (or 100 for slow).
- Drums: minimal — maybe kick only.
- FX reverb: size 0.6, mix 30%.
- FX delay: 1/8, mix 30%, fb 0.6.

## Goa / psytrance (1995-2001)

**Origin**: Goa, India (rave scene), then international. Astral Projection, Hallucinogen, Infected Mushroom.

**Tempo**: 140-150 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Fast melodic lines.
- Heavy slides.
- Many accents.
- High CUTOFF and RES.
- Often "modulated" (can't do this without external CC in acidflow).

**Drum style**:
- Four-on-floor kick, very hard.
- "Rolling" bass (psytrance has a sub-bass pulsing on every 16th — not possible in acidflow's single-voice setup).
- Snare on backbeat.

**FX**:
- Delays, phasers, many FX.
- Heavy processing.

**Key records**: *Hallucinogen - LSD*, *Astral Projection - Dancing Galaxy*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Squelch.
- Pattern archetype: Melodic.
- BPM: 145.
- Drums: hard kick, backbeat snare, 8th hats.

acidflow can approximate but not fully replicate psytrance — the genre depends on layered basslines that acidflow's single-voice setup doesn't support.

## Tech-house / acid-house revival (2000-present)

**Origin**: Josh Wink, Hot Since 82, Bicep, DMX Krew, Mike Servito.

**Tempo**: 120-128 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Balance of pedal and melodic.
- Moderate RES.
- Filter sweeps over long periods.

**Drum style**:
- Clean tech-house drums.
- Kick, clap, offbeat hats.
- Sometimes minimal snare.

**FX**:
- Tasteful. Subtle delay, some reverb.
- Clean mix.

**Arrangement**:
- Long builds, proper drops, long main sections.
- 6-8 minute tracks.

**Key records**: *Josh Wink - Higher State of Consciousness*, *Bicep - Glue*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Classic.
- Pattern archetype: Pedal or Melodic.
- BPM: 125.
- Drums: standard tech-house kit.
- FX: subtle delay and reverb.

## Modern acid techno (2010-present)

**Origin**: Hardfloor successors. Stay Up Forever, Berlin underground. Regal, SPFDJ, Volruptus.

**Tempo**: 135-150 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Aggressive, often self-oscillating.
- Dense patterns.
- Heavy drive and distortion.

**Drum style**:
- Pounding kick.
- Snare + clap layered.
- 16th-note hats.

**FX**:
- Heavy drive, maxed out.
- Delay for polyrhythm.
- Occasional reverb.

**Arrangement**:
- Relentless. Short intros, quickly to the main.
- Big drops.

**Key records**: *Regal - Acid Is My DNA*, *SPFDJ sets* (no specific track), *Volruptus - Dynamic Range*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Squelch.
- Pattern archetype: Driving.
- BPM: 145.
- Drums: full techno.
- FX drive: 60-80%.

## Dub-acid (2000-present)

**Origin**: Basic Channel lineage meets acid. Jeff Mills, DeepChord, Echospace.

**Tempo**: 110-125 BPM.

**303 style**:
- Very sparse.
- Long decays.
- Low RES.
- Filter motion over minutes.

**Drum style**:
- Dubby: kick, sub-occasional snare, maybe hats.
- Drums heavily reverberated.

**FX**:
- Heavy reverb.
- Moderate delay.

**Arrangement**:
- Slow evolution.
- Very long tracks.

**Key records**: Basic Channel catalog (though those aren't acid per se), DeepChord.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Knob archetype: Dubby.
- Pattern archetype: Dub.
- BPM: 120.
- Drums: minimal.
- FX reverb: size 0.7, mix 40%.

## Experimental acid (ongoing)

**Origin**: Always someone pushing limits. Luke Vibert, Ceephax Acid Crew, DMX Krew.

**Tempo**: variable, often outside dance tempos.

**303 style**:
- Unusual patterns.
- Deliberately atonal or weird.
- Self-oscillating used creatively.

**Drum style**:
- Unpredictable. Broken-beat, jazz-influenced, or no drums.

**FX**:
- Anything. Often heavy.

**Arrangement**:
- Non-standard. Could be sound collage.

**Key records**: *Luke Vibert - Acid Sessions*, *Ceephax Acid Crew - United Acid Emirates*.

**acidflow recipe**:
- Randomize heavily. Keep only surprising outputs.
- Don't follow any rules.

## How to identify a subgenre by ear

Quick checklist:

1. **Tempo**: under 128 = house; 128-140 = techno-ish; over 140 = harder subgenres.
2. **Kick intensity**: soft/boomy = house; hard/tight = techno.
3. **303 density**: sparse = Chicago/dub; dense = Hardfloor/techno.
4. **Filter motion**: slow = house/dub; fast/aggressive = hardfloor/techno.
5. **Drums**: clap-focused = acid house; snare-focused = techno; minimal = IDM/dub.
6. **FX**: clean = house; heavy = hardfloor; washy = dub.

Combine these and you can usually pin down the subgenre within 30 seconds of a record.

## When you don't know what you're making

It's fine to not fit a subgenre. Many great acid records don't cleanly classify. *Acid Tracks* is the origin of acid house but sounds different from what came after. The Plastikman tracks are weird enough that "ambient acid" doesn't fully capture them.

Don't force yourself into a subgenre. Make what you like. Let the genre label come later (or not at all).

## Try this

For each subgenre above:

1. Pick a reference record (your favorite one).
2. Listen to 30 seconds of it.
3. In acidflow, configure per the recipe.
4. Play.
5. Compare to the reference.

You won't match exactly (reference records have layers, mixing, mastering acidflow can't replicate). But the feel should be recognizable.

After cycling through all subgenres, you'll have a working vocabulary. When someone says "make it more Hardfloor" you'll know what to adjust.
